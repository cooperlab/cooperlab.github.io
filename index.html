Cooper Lab

Software from the Cooper Lab for Cancer Data Science at the Emory University School of Medicine.

Welcome

We are developing open-sourced software tools to help the cancer research community understand increasingly large and complex datasets. We are focused on the development of image analysis and machine learning algorithms to extract information from cancer images, and to use this information to gain scientific insights and to improve the classification of cancers for better prognostics. Our site, the Cancer Digital Slide Archive currently hosts more than 20TB of whole slide images of cancer associated with The Cancer Genome Atlas project. You can read more about this resource in our interview with the National Cancer Institute.

Please check our publications publications page for examples on how we apply these tools with our collaborators, including our recent review paper in Laboratory Investigation.

Active Learning

We are developing a web-based machine-learning system that enables biomedical researchers to train machine learning algorithms interactively on massive imaging datasets. This system uses active learning to provide a natural interaction between experts and learning algorithms with minimal knob tweaking, and very limited data labeling requirements. In this framework, the learning algorithms drive the selection of examples for human labeling, achieving better accuracy with less human effort. This work is funded by the National Library of Medicine.
